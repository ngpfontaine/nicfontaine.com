Creative Abstract

I have strong convictions on design and user-experience & a personal vendetta against slow, superfluous transitions, as well as illegibly low contrast — both rife in contemporary styles.

Coming from a background of late nights spent in powerful native applications, I prefer a direct, heavy-handed appproach to software design. That is — building out, around the most frequent actions and salient objectives of the user, as opposed to pulling in an opinionated front-end library as a templated project base or borrowing stylistic paradigms and calling them 'best practices'.

Beyond the CTAs, loading animations, and hero shots I prefer to prioritize the thousand other times a user will navigate an interface with an end goal in mind. And in development & testing I love finding the unique edge-cases that seem to only bloom in release — including the creativity & head bashing they bring, and the humbling experience of knowing you, again, came up short in the hypothetical storytelling of planning for every possibility. (And that night Jeff Goldblum visits you in your dreams to seductively explain chaos theory).
